RA Intensive Bootcamp · Summer 2026

A cohort-based AI bootcamp for research assistants

Learn to use AI tools across the full research assistant workflow — literature review, data exploration, code debugging, writing, and field coordination.

Dates and registration coming soon. Join a webinar in June to see our approach, or check back here for updates.

What this is

A live, cohort-based bootcamp taught by Emily Beam and Erkmen G. Aslim. Multiple sessions over several weeks, with homework exercises between sessions and a capstone project.

This is not a self-paced course or a recording you watch on your own. You’ll work through realistic RA tasks with AI tools, get feedback, and learn alongside a cohort of peers.

Who it’s for

  • Aspiring RAs and pre-docs who want to signal AI competence to PIs
  • Current RAs at research organizations (IPA, J-PAL, IDinsight, DIME, university labs) who want professional development

No prior AI experience required. You should be comfortable with at least one of Stata or R.

What you’ll learn

Each session focuses on a core RA workflow, with live demos and hands-on exercises.

📚

Literature Review

AI-assisted searching, summarizing, identifying gaps, and catching hallucinated citations.

📊

Data Exploration & Cleaning

Using AI to describe datasets, spot anomalies, and generate summary statistics — with verification guardrails.

💻

Code Assistance & Debugging

AI for Stata and R debugging, code translation, and documentation. Recognizing bad suggestions.

✍️

Writing & Field Coordination

Drafting memos, editing, survey instrument review, translation, and communicating with PIs and field teams.

🎯

Capstone

A multi-step RA task that brings it all together: dataset + papers → summary memo with descriptives and flagged issues.

Format

  • Live online sessions via Zoom or Teams
  • Homework between sessions — realistic RA tasks you complete with AI tools
  • Capstone exercise — demonstrate your skills on a multi-step research task
  • Cohort community — async channel for peer support and discussion
  • Certificate of completion upon finishing the program

Pricing

We use a sliding scale based on World Bank country income classifications to keep the program accessible globally.

Tier Price Who
High-income country $200–300 US, Europe, Australia, etc.
Middle-income country $75–100 Self-reported
Low-income country $25–50 Self-reported
Scholarship Free Application-based (limited spots)

Pricing is self-reported on the honor system. If your organization is paying, use the tier where the organization is headquartered.

Group rates: 10–15% discount for organizations sending 3+ participants.
